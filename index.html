The Last Best Place

The unoffcial nickname for Montana - the last best place. The origin of the nickname is disputed. It could be from the fact that Montana has an average of 7.4 people per square mile. Only Alaska and Wyoming have fewer people per square mile than Montana. As a reference, the national average is 75 people per square mile. Or the nickname could be because Montana still feels very wild and remote with large predators such as the grizzly bear. For Montanan's, it's the kind, hard-working, and helpful residents that make this state so special.
